Kolkata

Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, is the capital city of the Indian state of West Bengal. Located on the east bank of the Hooghly River, Kolkata is the second-largest city in India by population, with over 14 million inhabitants in the metropolitan area.

Some of the key aspects of Kolkata's cultural heritage are Literature and Art: Kolkata is known for its literary and artistic contributions. It has produced many famous writers, poets, and artists, including Rabindranath Tagore, Satyajit Ray, and Amartya Sen, who have made significant contributions to Indian and world culture. Music and Dance: Kolkata is also famous for its music and dance forms, including Rabindra Sangeet, which is a genre of songs composed by Rabindranath Tagore. Other popular dance forms include Kathakali, Bharatanatyam, and Manipuri. Festivals: Kolkata is known for its colorful festivals, which are celebrated with great enthusiasm and energy. The most famous festival is Durga Puja, which is a 10-day celebration in honor of the goddess Durga. Other festivals include Diwali, Holi, and Christmas. Food: Kolkata is famous for its food, which is a unique blend of Indian, British, and Chinese influences. Some of the popular dishes include roshogolla (a sweet made from cottage cheese), mishti doi (sweetened yogurt), and biryani (a spicy rice dish). Architecture: Kolkata has a rich architectural heritage, which reflects its colonial past. The Victoria Memorial, St. Paul's Cathedral, and the Howrah Bridge are some of the notable landmarks in the city. Sports: Kolkata is a sports-loving city, and it is home to several famous sports teams, including the Kolkata Knight Riders (cricket), Mohun Bagan and East Bengal (football), and the Bengal Warriors (kabaddi).

Bit more on Kolkata

It was once the center of British colonial administration in India, and many of its buildings and monuments reflect that legacy. The city has produced many famous writers, including Rabindranath Tagore, who won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1913. Kolkata is also famous for its street food, including puchkas (a type of spicy snack), jhal muri (a type of spiced puffed rice), and mishti doi (a sweet yogurt). The city is also home to several universities and research institutions, making it a hub of intellectual activity in India. The Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI), and the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) are some of the well-known academic institutions located in Kolkata.
